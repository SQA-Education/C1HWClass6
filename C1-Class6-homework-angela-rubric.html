1. Reading

From Side Hustle to Startup

Turning a side hustle into a real business requires more than enthusiasm. An entrepreneur has to understand customers, conduct market research, and decide whether the idea has scalability.

At an early stage, founders often prepare a pitch so they can attract investment. Once money begins to come in, they monitor revenue carefully and compare it to costs in order to understand profit.

A strategic founder might say: next month I will be researching the market, and by the end of the season I will be meeting potential partners. This kind of planning helps a business grow with intention rather than by chance.
